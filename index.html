About Me

  • I am a passionate learner about Earth Science and currently working on geophysical monitoring of permafrost thawing and Carbon dioxide injection as my PhD projects. I am a PhD candidate at INRS and based in a very beautiful city, in Canada. In 1608, this city was called Cape Diamond and now it is called Québec City (in French: Ville de Québec).
  • My journey in Earth Science has started a long time ago, when I was swimming in a hypersaline lake called Lake Urmia, in Iran and thinking how it is possile to see such a high saline lake in a city full of rain.
  • Long story short, my uyndergraduate studies were completed within 3 years in Petroleum exploration. My undergrad final project was about porosity and permeability estimation by integrating near and far offset seismic data. During Master studies, I worked on seismic data processing methods and my thesis was about mixed-phase wavelet estimation by optimizations methods such as Simulated Annealing. beside the seismic data processing project, I collaborated in side projects mainly geophysical data acquistions and in-situe processing of seismic data.
  • Currently, I work on geophysical monitoring of permafrost degredation as my PhD project. In this project, I am trying to understand the impact of thawing on electro-elastic properties of permafrost samples. In Phase 1 of this project, electro-acoustic measurements were done concurently by X-ray computed tomography (pixel size: 0.097 mm * 0.097 mm). In Phase 2, we moved to micro-properties investigation by conducting the same meausrements but by micro-CT scan (pixel size: 0.025 mm * 0.025 mm ).
  • During my PhD, I have the chance to collaborate with Geological Survey of Canada (GSC) in two projects:
    1- Geophyiscal data processing and interpretation of sedimentary deposits to detect hydrocarbon indicators and potential geohazards spots (Done project)
    2- Carbon Dioxide injection monitoring by simulatnous electro-acoustic measurements and micro-CT scanning (On-going project)

    • Projects

  • Permafrost degredation monitoring (On-going)

  • This is a laboratory macro-scale project. In this project, combinatorial measurements were conducted to measure the electro-acoustic properties of hand-made unconsolidated partially-frozen samples. The measurements were done during a cycle of thawing while medical CT scanning was operating to image the sample during phase transition from fully frozen to fully unfrozen state. To conduct the measurements, an exclusive set-up was designed and assembled.

  • During phase transition, pore content and pore network substantially changed. Hence, electro-elastic properties of the permafrost samples do not depend only on pore fluid transition. During thawing, P-wave velocity and attenuation drastically changes. while unfrozen increases, P-wave velocity reduces and attenuation increases. Partially frozen to fully unfrozen state transition can be determined by drastic reduction and increment of velocity and attenuation, respectively.

  • Permafrost Tuk samples monitoring (On-going)

    • This project is alos a laboratory project, but ar micro-scale. These samples were moved from west to east coast, new lab set-up has designed and assembled to investigate thawing impact on micro-properties of Tuk samples. This time, the samples will be monitored by micro-CT scan, by which, the pixel size of the scans will be approxiamtely 0.025 mm * 0.025 mm. This project is supported by Geological Survey of Canada
